📊 Telco Customer Churn Analysis
🔍 Project Overview

This project analyzes why customers leave a telecom company (customer churn).
The dataset includes 7,032 customers with details such as contract type, monthly charges, internet service, and payment method.

The main goal is to identify the key factors that lead to churn and suggest actions to improve customer retention.

🛠️ Tools & Technologies
Python (Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn) – Data cleaning and analysis
Power BI – Dashboard and data visualization
Dataset – IBM Telco Customer Churn Dataset (Kaggle)
📈 Key Findings
1. High Churn Rate

The overall churn rate is 26.58%, which means about 1 in 4 customers leave the company.
This indicates a serious customer retention issue.

2. Contract Type Matters

Customers with month-to-month contracts have a much higher churn rate compared to customers with yearly or long-term contracts.

3. New Customers Are at Risk

Customers in their first 10 months are more likely to leave.
Customers with longer tenure (especially above 70 months) rarely churn.

4. Higher Charges Increase Churn

Customers paying higher monthly charges (above $65) tend to leave more often.
This suggests a possible gap between price and perceived value.

5. Fiber Optic Users Have Higher Churn

Customers using fiber optic services show a higher churn rate, even though it is a premium service.
This may indicate pricing or service quality issues.

6. Payment Method Impact

Customers who use electronic check have higher churn rates.
Customers using automatic payments are less likely to leave.

💡 Business Recommendations
Encourage customers to switch to long-term contracts by offering discounts
Improve the onboarding experience for new customers (first 10 months)
Review fiber optic pricing and service quality
Promote automatic payment methods to improve retention
Provide loyalty offers for high-paying customers
