# telco-customer-churn-analysis
Customer churn analysis using Python and Power BI

Telco Customer Churn Analysis — Project Report
What is this project about?
This project analyzes why customers leave a telecom company. The dataset has 7,032 customers with 21 details about each one — like their contract type, monthly charges, internet service, and whether they left or stayed.
The goal is simple: find out who is leaving and why, so the company can fix it.

What I used

Python (Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn) — to clean the data and find patterns
Power BI — to build an interactive dashboard
Dataset — IBM Telco Customer Churn Dataset from Kaggle


What I found
1. Overall Churn Rate is 26.58%
That means 1 in 4 customers is leaving. That's high. Any telecom company would be worried about this number.
2. Month-to-month customers leave the most
Customers on monthly contracts churn way more than customers on one-year or two-year contracts. Makes sense — no commitment means easy to leave.
3. New customers leave faster
Customers in their first 10 months are the most likely to leave. After 70 months, almost nobody leaves. The longer someone stays, the more loyal they become.
4. Higher charges = more churn
Customers paying above $65/month churn more than customers paying less. They're probably feeling like they're not getting enough value for what they pay.
5. Fiber optic customers churn the most
Even though fiber optic is a premium service, these customers are leaving at a very high rate. The price is high but the satisfaction isn't matching it.
6. Electronic check users leave more
Customers who pay manually via electronic check churn significantly more than customers on automatic payments. Automatic payment customers rarely leave — probably because they don't think about it every month.

What the company should do

Push customers to switch from monthly to yearly contracts — offer discounts to make it worth it
Focus on keeping new customers happy in their first 10 months — strong onboarding, good support
Review fiber optic pricing — if customers are leaving, the price isn't matching the value
Encourage automatic payments — customers who autopay are less likely to cancel
Target high-paying customers with loyalty rewards before they decide to leave
